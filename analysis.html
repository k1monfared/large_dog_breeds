The Problem with Naive Trait Weighting

A straightforward approach to scoring "service dog suitability" would be to pick a list of relevant traits and weight them directly. For example: Easy To Train × 3 + Intelligence × 2 + …

But if two traits are highly correlated — say Easy To Train and Intelligence both have r = 0.85 — then they carry nearly the same signal. Weighting each one independently amplifies that shared signal and drowns out genuinely different information. The fix: compute the full Pearson correlation matrix, identify clusters of correlated traits, and replace each cluster with a single averaged signal before applying weights.
